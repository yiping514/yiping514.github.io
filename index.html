Who I am

A dad, a husband, a Christian, and a researcher in design optimization and Digital Twins.

Originally from Kaohsiung, Taiwan, I'm a postdoc in Mechanical Engineering at Northwestern University, advised by Dr. Wei Chen in the Integrated DEsign Automation Laboratory (IDEAL). My work integrates physics-based knowledge, machine learning surrogatesk and uncertainty quantification to enable efficient, reliable, and online-adaptable decision-making for Digital Twin systems. I will join the Department of Mechanical Engineering at National Taiwan University as a tenure-track assistant professor starting from February 1st, 2027.

Research

Learning systems that decide under uncertainty

My research applies data-driven methods to the core challenges of Digital Twins and design optimization under uncertainty — integrating machine learning as a surrogate for complex physics, and developing algorithms for real-time, online-adaptable decision-making. It spans two connected streams.

Uncertainty-Aware Real-Time Decision-Making in Digital Twins

Digital Twins are more than virtual replicas — they enable bi-directional interaction between a physical system and its digital counterpart. Integrating AI, uncertainty quantification, and real-time data, I develop methods for system design, performance optimization, and lifecycle management.

Read more →

Active Learning for Continual Learning & Sequential Decision-Making

Sequential decision-making is the key to active data collection for surrogate modeling and simulation-based design. By leveraging quantified model uncertainty, I design active-learning algorithms that maximize sampling efficiency in multi-fidelity design and Digital Twins.
